A tribute to the Zoetic

Many a times I wonder at the immensity of the universe spiralling its way out to the infinity beyond, the magnificence of the cosmic force unparalleled and unprejudiced, bringing our treacherous voyage for avarice and prominence to a standstill. And looking back to the reality laid bare in front of me, I can’t help but notice the same likeness of helplessness and admiration I felt for that heavenly abode to this city, which captured the heart and soul of the many millions out there, to see a remainder of their creator’s presence and relish that serene air to etch within them a permanence as eternal as the soul. Varanasi, the city of mystics, an epitome of spirituality, holy abode of lord Shiva, the harbinger of nirvana, the reminiscence of the heaven unknown, and many more are the epithets to describe this luxurious yet thought evoking place. As I walk into the limits I see the many faces telling a silent yet sublime story of a past once forgotten, the gaps being tugged at again and again by a force inexplicable, as I felt my own.

The treasure chest of authensity

Varanasi, the oldest city of India, does not stand just for the name alone, but it reflects a history so vast and rich, leaving the gapping to reinstall a sense of pride for the nation they live in. Varanasi, known among the natives as Banaras or Kashi, shines among the stars as bright as a supernova and begets a history of genesis and education. Bathed by the holy river, it’s a sight to behold; even 30,000 feet up the air, I could trace the Ganges clear and majestic leaving behind a trail of zing. Possibly coined from the names of the two rivers Varuna and Assi, the ancient city, from its transition to the modern scenario by the hands of a man named Mansa Ram to becoming a part of Uttar Pradesh after independence, reflects the advent of cultural revelation and has become the birthplace of many renowned figures, the likes of Tulsi Das, Prem chand, Kabir etc., who fought and lived for the cause and made a mark for the young to set forth. Sporting many a place to deem and lose oneself to the beauty of spiritualism and reality, I could just pick out those destinations blindfolded, which left a deep impression among the many who went the distance to feel the same.

1. The mighty Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Foremost of all holy destinations, Kashi Vishwanath temple stands out as a living epitome emanating a godly aura, attracting thousands of people across the nation and tourists from around the globe to pay homage to Lord Shiva and bask in his glory for the eternity the night offers. The long climb, along with the throbbing in the heart, gives an element of anticipation which overlooks the exhaustion of the body. The hymns narrating the grandeur of the lord and the everlasting will of humanity fill the divine hall, calming our burdened self, in the form of Aartis sung beautifully, devoid of separation of any form. The Ganga Aarti, that brightens the evening, brings about that serene scene back to its rightful place, an unearthly experience, which you have to see it to believe!

2. The Ghats

If there is a place as relaxing and relieving, the Ghats of Varanasi would treat you to feel the better half of that other haven. With 87 Ghats, the city glows high enough to touch the heaven in our heart! People of all religion and culture gather together to take part in this beautiful ceremony and pray homage to the Mighty River bringing peace and prosperity to her kin, to rinse oneself of all sins and greed; to move forward with a purpose, shinning the light known as knowledge to the world around. The most popular among all the Ghats in Varanasi are the Dasaswamedh Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, Harischandra Ghat, Kabir Ghat and Assi Ghat.

A pillar of education

Residing in the holiness of the soil is yet another great treasure whose foundation went back to pre-independence era; a seed sown with the better interest of our nation and its youth power, which bore a plethora of fruits and is ever expanding! The Banaras Hindu University (BHU), a tribute to the legacy of the origin and a panorama of education, stands tall and tells an epic tale, a captivating one of its founder Mahamana Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya Ji, his struggle to uplift the condition of India and to take it to its rightful stand among the superpowers. Sporting many disciplines, it’s a treasure house for all students to perceive their own path and become the human of tomorrow. Among the many institutions within this mighty university stands a tall figure sporting a long history of successful education, giving back a wealth of mighty figures, who made it to the other side. A walk inside the Indian Institute of Technology (Banaras Hindu University) makes one feel conscious about the general outline, the working circle holding the whole of it in check, the many students carrying their share of load with a stern yet satisfied face, the normal huff-buff going on about, general discussions on a sea of events and topics to relieve one off the worries about the extrinsic factors! Indeed the institute hums with activity each moment, pumping out new prospects each year for the better interest of the students and the faculty as whole. A history spanning the inception, pioneering, graduation, and finally integration of the disciplines to form the present day plot, the journey was indeed a laborious yet an amazing venture paving way for the tenacity we see today. Embodying 13 departments and 3 interdisciplinary schools, the institute provides a hub for nurturing skills and academic wealth of its gems. The buzz of student activities following their recess is quite refreshing to view; each involved in their own field of interest. The determined folks worked out, initiated and successfully brought to the surface many events at national level: SPARDHA, Kashiyatra, Technex to name a few, brought fame and highlighted the students to the professionals out there. With the ever expanding boundaries of disciplines and knowledge, IIT(BHU) has etched a place in Indian history and till date progresses with a target – to achieve the frontier of knowledge and share among all who wills it and aspire for a better My long awaited journey came to an end here, for this has become a place not much different from home I would say. As I stand in the soil and reminisce the past, I take a step forward with a smile on my face for the future; another landmark for a successful race has just begun.
